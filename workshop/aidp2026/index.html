Introduction

Recent advances in large-scale foundation models have fundamentally reshaped the landscape of artificial intelligence in digital pathology. By leveraging massive whole-slide image datasets and multimodal learning paradigms, these models have unlocked unprecedented capabilities in representation learning, cross-modal reasoning, and clinical decision support.

Building upon this paradigm shift, emerging directions including multi-omics integration, agent-based AI systems, and human-in-the-loop learning are further expanding the scope of computational pathology. These advances are accelerating the transition from proof-of-concept research toward scalable, real-world clinical deployment.

In this context, AIDP 2026 aims to foster interdisciplinary collaboration and drive the next generation of AI-powered pathology systems toward meaningful clinical impact.
