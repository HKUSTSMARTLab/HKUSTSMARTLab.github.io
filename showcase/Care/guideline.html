Digital Cytopathology and Artificial Intelligence Guidelines

A comprehensive set of guidelines for digital cytopathology implementation and artificial intelligence applications in cytopathology practice. This guideline includes three key publications that provide a roadmap for the future of digital cytopathology and AI integration.

Description: This editorial provides an overview of the current landscape and future directions for digital cytopathology and AI applications, serving as an introduction to the comprehensive guidelines presented in parts 1 and 2.

Clinical Cytopathology Guidelines

Standardized Reporting Systems in Cytopathology

Internationally recognized standardized reporting systems for various cytopathology specimens. These guidelines provide structured frameworks for diagnostic categorization, improving communication between pathologists and clinicians, and enhancing patient care.

Guidelines by Organ System:

Full Citation: Ali, S. Z., & VanderLaan, P. A. (2023). The Bethesda System for Reporting Thyroid Cytopathology: Definitions, Criteria, and Explanatory Notes. Springer Nature.

Publisher: Springer Nature

Description: The Bethesda System for Reporting Thyroid Cytopathology (TBSRTC) provides a standardized, six-tiered diagnostic framework for reporting thyroid fine-needle aspiration (FNA) results. This system improves communication between pathologists and clinicians, facilitates cytologic-histologic correlation, and supports clinical management decisions.

Key Categories:

  • I: Nondiagnostic or Unsatisfactory
  • II: Benign
  • III: Atypia of Undetermined Significance (AUS) or Follicular Lesion of Undetermined Significance (FLUS)
  • IV: Follicular Neoplasm or Suspicious for a Follicular Neoplasm
  • V: Suspicious for Malignancy
  • VI: Malignant
Access Guideline ISBN: 978-3-031-28046-7

Full Citation: Nayar, R., & Wilbur, D. C. (2015). The Bethesda System for Reporting Cervical Cytology: Definitions, Criteria, and Explanatory Notes. Springer.

Publisher: Springer

Description: The Bethesda System for Reporting Cervical Cytology (TBS) provides a standardized framework for reporting cervical/vaginal cytologic diagnoses. This system facilitates clear communication between the laboratory and clinician, promoting effective patient management and monitoring disease trends in patient populations.

Key Categories:

  • Specimen Adequacy
  • General Categorization
  • Interpretation/Result (including Negative for Intraepithelial Lesion or Malignancy, Epithelial Cell Abnormalities, and Other)
Access Guideline ISBN: 978-3-319-11074-5

Full Citation: Wojcik, E. M., Kurtycz, D. F. I., & Rosenthal, D. L. (2022). The Paris System for Reporting Urinary Cytology (Vol. 2). Springer.

Publisher: Springer

Description: The Paris System for Reporting Urinary Cytology (TPS) provides a standardized international system for reporting urinary tract cytopathology with a focus on detecting high-grade urothelial carcinoma. This system improves the sensitivity and specificity of urinary cytology reporting and enhances communication between pathologists and urologists.

Key Categories:

  • I: Nondiagnostic/Unsatisfactory
  • II: Negative for High-Grade Urothelial Carcinoma
  • III: Atypical Urothelial Cells
  • IV: Suspicious for High-Grade Urothelial Carcinoma
  • V: High-Grade Urothelial Carcinoma
  • VI: Other Malignancies, Primary and Metastatic, and Miscellaneous Lesions
Access Guideline ISBN: 978-3-030-88686-8

Full Citation: Chandra, A., Crothers, B., Kurtycz, D., & Schmitt, F. (2019). Announcement: the international system for reporting serous fluid cytopathology. Acta cytologica, 63(5), 349-351. S. Karger AG.

Publisher: S. Karger AG

Description: The International System for Serous Fluid Cytopathology provides a standardized approach to the cytologic evaluation of pleural, pericardial, and peritoneal effusions. This system aims to improve diagnostic accuracy and communication between pathologists and clinicians.

Key Categories:

  • I: Nondiagnostic
  • II: Negative for Malignancy
  • III: Atypia of Undetermined Significance
  • IV: Suspicious for Malignancy
  • V: Malignant
Access Guideline DOI: 10.1159/000500449

Full Citation: Field, A. S., Raymond, W. A., Rickard, M., Arnold, L., Brachtel, E. F., Chaiwun, B., Chen, L., Di Bonito, L., Kurtycz, D. F. I., Lee, A. H. S., et al. (2019). The international academy of cytology Yokohama system for reporting breast fine-needle aspiration biopsy cytopathology. Acta Cytologica, 63(4), 257-273.

Publisher: S. Karger AG

Description: The International Academy of Cytology Yokohama System provides a standardized approach to reporting breast fine-needle aspiration biopsy cytopathology. This system aims to improve diagnostic accuracy, reproducibility, and clinical utility of breast FNA.

Key Categories:

  • 1: Insufficient Material
  • 2: Benign
  • 3: Atypical, Probably Benign
  • 4: Suspicious, Probably Malignant
  • 5: Malignant
Access Guideline DOI: 10.1159/000499509

Full Citation: Pitman, M. B., Centeno, B. A., Reid, M. D., Siddiqui, M. T., Layfield, L. J., Perez-Machado, M., Weynand, B., Stelow, E. B., Lozano, M. D., Fukushima, N., et al. (2023). The World Health Organization reporting system for pancreaticobiliary cytopathology. Acta Cytologica, 67(3), 304-320.

Publisher: S. Karger AG

Description: The World Health Organization (WHO) reporting system for pancreaticobiliary cytopathology provides a standardized terminology and nomenclature system for pancreaticobiliary cytology specimens. This system has been incorporated into the World Health Organization (WHO) classification and aims to improve communication and patient management.

Key Categories:

  • I: Nondiagnostic
  • II: Negative (for Malignancy)
  • III: Atypical
  • IV: Neoplastic (Benign and Other)
  • V: Suspicious (for Malignancy)
  • VI: Positive/Malignant
Access Guideline DOI: 10.1159/000527912

Full Citation: Schmitt, F. C., Bubendorf, L., Canberk, S., Chandra, A., Cree, I. A., Engels, M., Hiroshima, K., Jain, D., Kholová, I., Layfield, L., et al. (2023). The World Health Organization reporting system for lung cytopathology. Acta Cytologica, 67(1), 80-91.

Publisher: S. Karger AG

Description: The World Health Organization (WHO) reporting system for lung cytopathology provides a standardized approach to the cytologic evaluation of lung specimens. This system aims to improve diagnostic accuracy, reproducibility, and communication between pathologists and clinicians, incorporating the latest advances in molecular pathology.

Key Features:

  • Integration of cytomorphology with molecular findings
  • Standardized reporting terminology
  • Guidance on specimen adequacy and preparation
  • Diagnostic criteria for major lung tumor types
Access Guideline DOI: 10.1159/000527580

Full Citation: IAC-IARC-WHO Joint Editorial Board. (2024). WHO Reporting System for Lymph Node, Spleen, and Thymus Cytopathology. IAC-IARC-WHO Cytopathology Reporting Systems, 1st Edition, Volume 3.

Publisher: International Agency for Research on Cancer (IARC)

Description: The WHO Reporting System for Lymph Node, Spleen, and Thymus Cytopathology provides a standardized approach to the cytologic evaluation of lymphoid tissues. This system aims to improve diagnostic accuracy, reproducibility, and communication between pathologists and clinicians in the diagnosis of lymphoid disorders.

Key Features:

  • Standardized terminology for lymph node cytopathology
  • Integration with histopathologic and molecular classifications
  • Guidance on specimen adequacy and preparation
  • Diagnostic criteria for reactive and neoplastic lymphoid processes
Access Guideline ISBN: 978-92-832-0462-5

Full Citation: Faquin, W. C., Rossi, E. D., Baloch, Z., Barkan, G. A., Foschini, M. P., Kurtycz, D. F. I., Pusztaszeri, M. P., & Vielh, P. (2023). The Milan System for Reporting Salivary Gland Cytopathology. Springer.

Publisher: Springer

Description: The Milan System for Reporting Salivary Gland Cytopathology (MSRSGC) provides a standardized, tiered approach to the cytologic evaluation of salivary gland lesions. This system aims to improve communication between pathologists and clinicians, enhance the diagnostic accuracy of salivary gland FNA, and guide clinical management.

Key Categories:

  • I: Non-diagnostic
  • II: Non-neoplastic
  • III: Atypia of Undetermined Significance (AUS)
  • IVA: Benign Neoplasm
  • IVB: Salivary Gland Neoplasm of Uncertain Malignant Potential (SUMP)
  • V: Suspicious for Malignancy
  • VI: Malignant
Access Guideline ISBN: 978-3-031-26662-1

Full Citation: Layfield, L. J., & Baloch, Z. (2019). The Papanicolaou Society of Cytopathology System for Reporting Respiratory Cytology. Cham: Springer.

Publisher: Springer

Description: The Papanicolaou Society of Cytopathology System for Reporting Respiratory Cytology provides a standardized approach to the cytologic evaluation of respiratory tract specimens. This system aims to improve diagnostic accuracy, reproducibility, and communication between pathologists and clinicians in the diagnosis of respiratory tract lesions.

Key Features:

  • Standardized terminology for respiratory cytopathology
  • Risk stratification for malignancy
  • Guidance on specimen adequacy and preparation
  • Integration with molecular testing recommendations
  • Management recommendations based on cytologic diagnosis
Access Guideline ISBN: 978-3-319-97235-0

Source: Various international cytopathology societies and the World Health Organization
